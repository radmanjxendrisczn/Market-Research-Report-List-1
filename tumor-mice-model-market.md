<p><p>The tumor mice model market consists of various types used for cancer research. Humanized immune system mice models possess human immune cells, enabling the study of human-specific tumors. Syngeneic tumor mice models use genetically identical cells to study tumor growth in a controlled environment. Immunodeficient mice models lack an immune system, making them suitable for xenograft studies. NOG mice models are highly immunodeficient, enhancing graft success. Spontaneous tumor mice models develop tumors naturally. Additional models include genetically modified and transgenic mice, facilitating diverse research applications.</p></p>
