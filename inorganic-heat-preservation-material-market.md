<p><p>The inorganic heat preservation material market includes various types, each offering unique insulation properties. Empty vitrified beads are lightweight and provide thermal resistance. Expanded perlite, made from volcanic glass, offers excellent insulation and soundproofing. Closed cell perlite features a denser structure, enhancing thermal performance. Rockwool, derived from volcanic rock, is known for its fire resistance and acoustic insulation. Foamed concrete combines lightness and durability, serving as an effective insulator in construction applications, promoting energy efficiency and sustainability.</p></p>
