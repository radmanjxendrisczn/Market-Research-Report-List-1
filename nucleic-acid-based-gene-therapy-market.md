<p><p>The nucleic acid-based gene therapy market comprises several types: </p><p>1. **Anti-Sense and Anti-Gene Therapies** target specific RNA molecules to inhibit gene expression.</p><p>2. **Short Inhibitory Sequences (siRNA)** specifically degrade target mRNA, preventing protein synthesis. </p><p>3. **Gene Transfer Therapy** introduces therapeutic genes into patients' cells to treat genetic disorders. </p><p>4. **Nucleoside Analogs** mimic natural nucleotides, disrupting viral replication. </p><p>5. **Ribozymes** are RNA molecules that catalyze chemical reactions to alter gene expression. </p><p>6. **Aptamers** bind to specific proteins or nucleic acids, blocking their functions. </p><p>7. **Others** include emerging technologies and hybrid techniques in gene modulation.</p></p>
