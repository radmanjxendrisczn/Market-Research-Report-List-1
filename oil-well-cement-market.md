<p><p>The oil-well cement market comprises three primary types: Ordinary, Moderate Sulfate-Resistant, and High Sulfate-Resistant cement. Ordinary cement is commonly used for standard applications but may not withstand aggressive environments. Moderate Sulfate-Resistant cement offers enhanced resistance to sulfate attacks, making it suitable for areas with limited exposure to corrosive materials. High Sulfate-Resistant cement provides superior protection against severe sulfate conditions, ensuring durability in harsh environments, particularly in offshore and deep-well applications where the risk of sulfate attack is significant.</p></p>
